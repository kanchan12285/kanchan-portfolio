Project Stories

How each project came together

A closer look at three projects — the brief, the approach, and how it got built.

callfx.com
Fintech · CallFX

A trading platform that needed to feel institutional

The brief was to build a professional website for a UK-registered forex broker. In financial services, the first thing a visitor evaluates isn't the design — it's whether the site feels trustworthy enough to put money near. That shaped every decision.

The site needed to cover forex pairs, commodities, indices, crypto, multiple account types, and an education section — all without overwhelming visitors. I structured the navigation so that different types of users (existing traders vs. new prospects) could each find what they needed without friction.

Built using WordPress and Elementor, with attention to page hierarchy, consistent section layouts, and a design that could scale as the broker adds more market offerings.

sohent.in
E-Commerce · Sohent

Getting a natural foods brand ready to sell online

Sohent sells cold-pressed oils, premium spices, and seeds — products where trust in quality matters as much as the price. The challenge was building an online store that reflected that quality rather than looking like a generic WooCommerce template.

I set up the full WooCommerce infrastructure: product categories, individual product pages, a cart and checkout flow, and a first-order discount that activates automatically. The slider-based homepage made it easy to feature seasonal products or promotions without touching the code.

The design palette stayed earthy and clean — nothing that would distract from the product photography or undercut the brand's "healthy and natural" positioning.

NextStep App
Android · NextStep App

A learning tracker that doesn't feel like homework

NextStep was my TYCs project — a full Android application built to help students track learning goals while staying engaged through mini-games. The brief I set myself was simple: make something a student would actually open more than once.

The entire UI was designed and built independently using Kotlin and XML. Firebase Authentication handles sign-in, and Cloud Firestore stores user progress in real time. The mini-game layer was where the interesting UX decisions lived — figuring out how to reward progress without turning the tracker into a distraction.

The project gave me hands-on experience with mobile UI patterns, real-time data sync, and building something end-to-end without a team.

How I work

From first call to live website

A consistent approach that keeps projects moving without unnecessary back-and-forth.

01
🔍
Discovery
Understanding the business, its users, and what the website actually needs to do — before touching a theme or plugin.
